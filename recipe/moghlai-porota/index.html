Moghlai porota

Jul 28, 2016 in recipe

A deep-fried pastry with a filling of keema and beaten eggs

Making moghlai porota at home may seem like a daunting task. And we won’t lie, it does require a fair bit of planning. But for the moghlai porota aficionado, the pay-offs are enormous!

The moghlai porota is essentially a flaky flour pastry filled with a mixture of beaten eggs, keema, spices, and seasoning. It differs from other stuffed porotas in that the filling here is not dry, but wet and runny (because of the beaten eggs). For this reason, the porota must be sealed very well if you don’t want the egg spilling out into the oil when you fry.

This recipe might seem daunting. But the pay-offs are enormous! What you need foremost while making the moghlai porota is organisation. Plan for all these elements individually, and in this order:

  1. The keema—We like to use cooked mutton or beef keema. However, if you use chicken keema, you will be able to get away with using it raw. It will cook while the porota is frying. Whichever you choose, prepare the keema ahead of time.
  2. The dough—This will form the pastry within which the egg filling is enclosed. Be sure to knead the flour and set it aside for at least 30 minutes.
  3. The filling—While your flour is resting, you have some time to gather and prep ingredients for the filling. This is when you want to chop the onions, green chillies, ginger, and coriander. Toast some peanuts and roughly crush them. Get the bread crumbs and salt. Beat all these ingredients with the egg and keep it ready.
  4. The sides—Cut up a salad of cucumber and beets. If you have some leftover kosha mangsho or chicken curry, go heat it up. Squeeze some ketchup on the serving plate. It is now ready to receive the moghlai porota straight as it comes out of the oil!
  5. Rolling the porota—Clear a large surface like a kitchen counter or dining table. Oil the rolling pin and the surface well. Start rolling the dough. You want to roll it into a rectangular sheet, about 2mm thick.
  6. Bringing it all together—Place the filling at the centre of the rolled sheet. Fold the pastry and seal it like an envelope. Deep fry the moghlai porota, drain off the excess oil and bring it to you chopping board. Cut into four or nine squares, and serve.

Cooking time 60 minutes
Yield 1 porota

Ingredients

FOR THE PASTRY
| Quantity | Ingredient |
|———:|—————————————|
| 75 g | Flour |
| 1 g | Salt |
| 1 g | Sugar |
| 5 g | Oil |
| 40 g | Water |
FOR THE FILLING
| Quantity | Ingredient |
|———:|—————————————|
| 1 piece | Egg (beaten) |
| 20 g | Keema (cooked mutton or beef keema ) |
| 10 g | Onions (finely chopped) |
| 2 g | Green chillies (finely chopped) |
| 2 g | Ginger (finely chopped) |
| 3 g | Coriander (finely chopped) |
| 5 g | Peanuts (toasted and roughly crushed) |
| 10 g | Bread crumbs |
| 2 g | Salt |

Equipment

  • Mixing bowl or dish
  • Rolling pin | Large rolling surface
  • Frying pan
  • Frying spoon | jhhajhri

Appliances

  • Stove

Method

Knead the dough

  1. Place the flour, salt sugar, and oil in the mixing bowl and distribute the ingredients evenly.
  2. Add warm water and knead the dough for no less than five minutes.
  3. Coat the dough with 2 tablespoons of oil and set aside for 30 minutes.

Prepare the filling

  1. Finely chop the onions, ginger, green chillies, and coriander.
  2. Lightly toast the peanuts in a pan till they are lightly coloured, and crush them roughly.
  3. Now, crack an egg in a bowl. Add the cooked keema, onions, ginger, green chillies, coriander, peanuts, breads crumbs, and salt.
  4. Beat till all the ingredients are well combined.

Roll the pastry

  1. Oil your rolling surface and rolling pin.
  2. Place the rested dough on the surface and start rolling.
  3. In the beginning, when the pastry is small, you may rotate it between two rolls. But as the pastry stretches and increases in size, you may have to change the direction in which YOU roll, while keeping the pastry stationary on the surface. This is why it helps to have a large clear surface (like a dining table) to work on.
  4. The dough needs to be rolled into a rectangular sheet, 30cm by 40cm large.

Bring it all together

  1. Place the filling in the centre of the rolled pastry.
  2. We must now form an envelope so that the pastry encloses the filling completely.
  3. For this, start by folding the longer sides first. Press down gently to seal.
  4. The fold the shorter sides such that the envelope is closed. Press to seal.
  5. Lower the porota into hot oil and fry on both sides till golden brown. Pat down with the frying spoon to make sure that the filling gets cooked.
  6. Remove from the oil and place it on a chopping board. Cut it into square segments, and serve.

Served with

  • Alu’r torkari
  • Chicken kosha
  • Salad
  • Ketchup/chilli sauce
